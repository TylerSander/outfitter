About

Setting up a computer shouldn't mean twenty tabs, twenty installers, and a prayer that none of them were fakes. Outfitter is one shelf of good software, installed the way your operating system already trusts.

What it does

Curated, not crowded

A hand-picked shelf of the apps people actually want — browsers, chat, games, dev tools, AI tools — with real descriptions and real logos. No ads, no bundleware, no ★★★★★ lookalikes.

Provenance on every tile

Every app shows exactly where it comes from — "via winget · VideoLAN.VLC" — and community-maintained packages are labeled as such. You always know what you're installing.

Updates itself

Outfitter checks its own releases and updates in place with one click, verified with cryptographic signatures before anything runs.

Your list, everywhere

Sign in to save links and apps you want on your next machine. Set up a new computer by opening Outfitter and pressing install.

How it works

Browse the shelf

Pick from a categorized catalog of real apps with honest descriptions — or search for the one you came for.

Outfitter asks your OS

Behind the scenes it hands the job to your platform's own package manager: winget on Windows, Homebrew on macOS, Flatpak on Linux.

Installed from the source

The package manager downloads the publisher's official release and installs it — the same as if you'd typed the command yourself.

What we don't do

Outfitter never hosts installers and never re-wraps anyone's software. There is no telemetry, no sponsored placement, and nothing to upsell. The whole thing is open source, and the app's own updates are cryptographically signed and verified before a single byte runs. Where a listing is maintained by the community rather than the publisher, the tile says so — provenance first, always.
